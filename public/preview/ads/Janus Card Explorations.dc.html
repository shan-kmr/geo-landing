Then: the depth ladder (how people go deeper) and the POI prominence system (how the famous places surface, with receipts).

VERDICT — THEY STACK
These aren't rivals, they're strata of one card: 3a's lede + comparison figures open the card, 3c's anchor dossier names the place, 3d's hour ruler sits above the fold, and 3b's distribution strips become the row style inside the Desk-02 ledger. Reading order: sentence → names → hour → instruments → ledger. Ship 3b's strips first (pure component swap), then the anchor block, then the lede, then the ruler.
GOING DEEPER — THE DEPTH LADDER five rungs, one gesture each · nothing opens a new page until rung 5

"Deeper" must never mean "more rows." Each rung answers a different question, and each is one tap from the last. This is the whole interaction model of the card — the four designs above are skins over this ladder.

POIS, ALIVE — PROMINENCE WITH RECEIPTS "famous" is not a score — it's a count of independent evidence, shown

Do we have signal on which places are the big ones? Yes — four independent ones already in the stack, plus one to license. A place is an ANCHOR when ≥3 signals agree, NAMED when the world has written it down at all, FABRIC otherwise (counted, never ranked). Prominence is expressed by type size and ink weight only — the signal color still belongs to what's alive.

The anti-rule: no composite "prominence score" is ever computed or stored. The tier is a threshold on evidence count; the receipts themselves are what the user sees. A place can't buy a tier — it can only be sworn into one.

ALIVE, NOT ANIMATED
Anchors carry their pulse into the card: the station's swipe curve is the hour machine's spine; the market's visits drive the footfall strip. When the live layer runs, an anchor with a bus at its door shows it — M55 at the corner · now. Fame decays too: receipts carry vintages, and a closed place drops to FABRIC at the next build.
