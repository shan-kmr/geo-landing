WHY
The rail replaces the select. Seven shades, one keystroke each (1–7, 0 = none). The open rail shows what the ink currently means; the dropdown hid it behind a click.
Annotation over legend. Turning a shade on posts a sentence, not a colorbar: name · unit · denominator · vintage, with an × to clear.

PLATES — AUTHORED VIEWS WITH A THESIS ○ proposed · named like plates in a printed atlas — because that is what they are

A plate is a camera, a shade, optionally a cell and a live toggle — plus one sentence that says what you are looking at. Plates are authored (by Janus, later by customers), never auto-generated. They make return visits land somewhere with intent, and they make sales demos reproducible.

URL SCHEMA — THE STATE IS THE ADDRESS
/atlas/?h=892a1072cb3ffff cell → camera lands on it, card opens &shade=wt_visit_count ink meaning; absent = structure &lens=underwriting ledger sort preset (a|b|c…) &live=0 live layer off (default on) &plate=canal-st-8am authored view — see precedence &cam=14.6,40.719,-74.001,-12,48 z,lat,lon,bearing,pitch
precedence: plate < cam < h (most specific wins) · unknown params ignored · every param optional · URL mirrors state via replaceState, debounced 300 ms · plates resolve from /plates.json: { slug, name, thesis, cam, shade, h?, live? }
PLATE RAIL BEHAVIOR
Rail sits bottom-left, collapsed to three names + ⋯. Hover a chip → its thesis line appears above the rail. Click → 1.4 s authored flight (never a cut), shade crossfades en route.
Keyboard: [ and ] step through plates in authored order — a slideshow for demos, one keystroke per scene.
A plate never traps: any manual camera move exits plate state (the chip un-fills), and the URL swaps &plate= for &cam=.

The card becomes a bottom sheet. Peek = id + the lens headline number (96 px). Drag to 60% for the ledger; map stays live above.
Tap replaces hover. First tap selects (ring + peek), second opens. The hover contract's words move into the peek.
Ladder unchanged. Pinch zooms; tapping a coarse cell still flies one rung. Shade rail collapses to the caption chip; tapping it opens a sheet of the seven shades.
Plates become the mobile home. On small screens the rail is the first thing offered — reading an authored view beats freeform exploration on a phone.
Not ported: lens chips (URL only), keyboard stepping, tooltip. Hit targets ≥ 44 px throughout.
MOTION — EVERY TRANSITION IN THE PRODUCT
fade never pop · one easing family · all of it optional
{{ m.what }} {{ m.dur }} {{ m.ease }} {{ m.note }}
prefers-reduced-motion: camera flights become two-step fades (out at source, in at target); tile splits and panel slides become instant state changes; buses jump per GPS fix; the approach sequence plays as captions. No meaning is carried by motion alone — motion is emphasis, words are the content.
PRINT — the Atlas prints like a plate from a printed atlas: hairlines hold at 300 dpi, the ramp is grayscale-true, live vehicles freeze at last fix with a timestamp caption, and the ring prints as the page's single non-structural mark. If a screen fails this test, the screen is wrong.
